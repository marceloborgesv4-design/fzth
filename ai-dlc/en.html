Many teams already use AI to write code. Few have rethought how they plan, build and operate software once AI becomes part of the end-to-end workflow — and that is where most of the value is left on the table.

The AI-Driven Development Lifecycle (AI-DLC) is an AI-native methodology, already adopted by AWS customers, that brings AI in as an active collaborator across every stage of development, not just coding. It is not tied to a specific tool: it works with whatever AI coding assistant your team already uses or plans to adopt (Amazon Kiro, GitHub Copilot, Claude Code, and others).

In practice, AI-DLC works across three fronts:

Inception — AI analyzes requirements, flags gaps and edge cases, and helps draft architectures that your architects refine.

Construction — it accelerates code generation, testing and pull-request review, freeing the team for the harder problems.

Operation — AI agents support monitoring, incident triage and continuous improvement.

For the business, this translates into faster releases, higher code quality with defects caught earlier, traceability that is audit- and compliance-ready, and results in both greenfield and existing systems.
